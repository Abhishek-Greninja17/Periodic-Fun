Features of Periodic Table:

  • Groups: The Periodic Table has 18 Vertical Columns (groups)
  • arranged in left to right: IA-(1), IIA-(2), IIIB-(3), IVB-(4), VB-(5), VIB-(6), VIIB-(7), VIII-(8, 9, 10), IB-(11), IIB-(12), IIIA-(13), IVA-(14), VA-(15), VIA-(16), VIIA-(17), Zero-(18)
  • Groups can be determined by the number of outermost shell.
  • Group-1 : Alkali Metals
  • Group-2 : Alkaline Earth Metals
  • Group-(3-12) : Transition Elements
  • Group-13 : Boron Family
  • Group-14 : Carbon Family
  • Group-15 : Nitrogen Family
  • Group-16 : Oxygrn Family
  • Group-17 : Halogen Family
  • Group-18 (zero) : Nobel Gases or inert gases
  • Periods: The Periodic Table has 7 horizontal Rows (Periods)
  • Number of shells determines period
  • 1st Period: 2 elements- shortest period
  • 2nd & 3rd Period: 8 elements each- short period :
  • Bridge elements-(shows diagonal relationships in properties)
  • Typical elements: 3rd period elements summarise properties of respective groups
  • 4th & 5th Period: 18 elements each
  • 6th Period: 32 element- longest period : [Group 3 of 6th period: Lanthanides(La-Lu)]
  • 7th Period: incomplete Period [Group 3 of 7th Period : Actinides(Ac-Lr) ]

Periodicity:

  • Def: Property repeated after regular intervals
  • Cause: Recurrance of Simillar Electronic Configuration
  • * The electron configuration of an element describes how electrons are distributed in its atomic orbitals

Orbits and Valancy:

  • Def Orbits(Shells): Certain Path formed by the probability of availibity of eletrons revolving around nucleus [path of e-]
  • Trend: Increases down the group and Across the Period remains same
  • Def Valancy: Combining capacity of atom of an element i.e, no. of e- accepted or donated by atom
  • Generally: if outermost shell has 1,2,3,4 electrons then valancy is +1, +2, +3, +4 respectively else 8-(no. of electrons)
  • Actually: some have multiple valancies
  • Trend: usually down the group remains same and across the period first increases then decreases
